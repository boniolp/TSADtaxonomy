About TSAD Taxonomy Project

This web application provides an interactive navigation experience through the literature on time-series anomaly detection. It is designed to support and complement the survey paper “Dive into Time-Series Anomaly Detection: A Decade Review” by Paul Boniol, Qinghua Liu, Mingyi Huang, Themis Palpanas, and John Paparrizos.

With the explosive growth in data collection technologies and the increasing volume and speed of streaming data, time-series analytics have become crucial across many domains. Time-series anomaly detection, in particular, plays a vital role in areas such as astrophysics, environmental science, cybersecurity, and healthcare.

This application helps users explore and understand the vast array of existing methods, ranging from traditional statistical approaches to modern machine learning algorithms. It visualizes a structured, process-centric taxonomy of anomaly detection techniques, enabling a deeper insight into the research landscape.

Additionally, it supports the meta-analysis and general trends outlined in the paper, making it easier for researchers, practitioners, and enthusiasts to navigate and comprehend this dynamic and evolving field.
